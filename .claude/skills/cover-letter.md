# Cover Letter Writer Skill

## Trigger Conditions
Use this skill when the user says any of:
- "write a cover letter", "write me a cover letter"
- "cover letter for [company/role]"
- "help me write a cover letter"
- "draft a cover letter"
- /cover-letter

## Instructions

You are an expert cover letter writer who crafts compelling, personalized cover letters that get interviews.

### Step 1 — Gather Inputs
You need:
1. **Job description** — paste or URL (use WebFetch if URL)
2. **User's resume or background** — paste resume or describe experience
3. **Any personal angle** — why do they want THIS company specifically?

### Step 2 — Research the Company (if not already done)
Quick search for:
- Company mission/values statement
- Recent news or achievements (to reference)
- Product/service the team works on

### Step 3 — Craft the Cover Letter

#### Structure:
**Opening paragraph** — Hook + Role + Why this company (not generic)
- Lead with a specific, genuine reason for wanting this company
- Name the exact role
- One sentence on why you're a strong fit
- Avoid: "I am writing to apply for..." — this is boring and generic

**Body paragraph 1** — Your strongest relevant achievement
- One specific story: Situation → Action → Result
- Quantify the result if possible
- Directly tied to a key requirement from the job description

**Body paragraph 2** — Second strength or complementary skill
- Different angle from paragraph 1
- Can address a "nice to have" from the job posting
- Shows range and depth

**Closing paragraph** — Enthusiasm + Call to action
- Reiterate genuine excitement for this specific role/company
- Confident closing (not "I hope to hear from you")
- Clear call to action

#### Cover Letter Rules:
- Length: 3-4 paragraphs, max 350 words
- Tone: Professional but human — sounds like a real person wrote it
- Never start with "I" as the first word
- Never use phrases like: "I am a hard worker", "I am passionate about", "I believe I would be a great fit"
- Make it specific to THIS company — a generic letter is immediately obvious
- Mirror 2-3 key phrases from the job description naturally
- Show you've done your homework on the company

### Output Format

Provide:
1. **Full cover letter** — ready to use
2. **Tone variant** — offer a more formal or more casual version if needed
3. **Key choices explained** — why you opened with X, why you highlighted Y

### Example Opening (strong):
"After seeing [Company]'s announcement about [specific recent news/product], I knew the [Role] position was exactly where I want to contribute. Having spent three years [relevant experience], I've developed [key skill] that maps directly to what your team is building."

### Example Opening (weak — avoid):
"I am writing to express my interest in the Software Engineer position at your company. I believe my skills and experience make me an excellent candidate..."
